Professional Development Plan

  • Three areas for professional growth and development:
    • Select appropriate interventions methods for a client-centered plan regarding healing principles, orthotic devices and diverse disease stages/processes
    • Become more confident in my knowledge on assistive technology, as technology is the future or the healthcare field and will continue to evolve and get more complex
    • Use evidence-based approaches to provide safe and competent services to all clients
  • Five strategies for success and indicate how these will contribute to professional growth:
    • Taking notes on all the readings during my last semester will help me understand the content more, thus enabling me to feel more confident to plan client-centered interventions
    • Researching the different modalities and their use and success in the clinical setting will help me gain more knowledge on how to use them in practice and for what diagnosis/condition to increase evidence-based practice
    • Time management with the help of my planner will help me stay more organized and on top of the deadlines of my assignments this semester, therefore helping me to stay more focused on thinking my assignments through carefully to enhance my clinical reasoning, which is needed for appropriate intervention planning in the future
    • Going to OT specific conferences/events, such as the annual AOTA conference, to increase my understanding of evidence-based practice, assistive technology and all the advances that have been made in the OT field to provide client-centered service in the future
    • Putting together a binder with all information that is important regarding healing phases, disease processes, orthotics, assessments and clinical conditions to take with me on fieldwork to prepare myself better for the clinical experience and to have a resource ready as a reference to make my time on fieldwork more successful
  • Three potential obstacles or challenges to achieving professional goals:
    • The OT field does not have enough current research available for modalities in association with certain diagnosis/conditions to always be able to provide evidence-based services
    • The technology is changing so quickly these days that it is hard to keep up with all of the newest advances on top of a busy workload. Furthermore, the OT might not always be able to get first-hand experience of some of the newest technology on the market and will more likely recommend the technology that works currently, even if other options might be more appropriate in certain cases. The student in this case will only get a certain amount of insight into the technology that the children of the A&D waiver program use, and not necessarily into the newest technology available.
    • The student/OT might not always find enough time available to go to conferences or events to learn about the newest advances in the field and enhance the knowledge of client-centered interventions due to personal obligations or even work obligations.
